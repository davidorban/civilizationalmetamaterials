## Purpose

You are being invited to participate in a research study. Please read this
form carefully before deciding whether to participate.

This study examines how information provenance and verification practices
affect the quality and coordination of grant review decisions. You are being
invited because you are a member of a grant review panel that has been
selected for this study.

---

## What will happen

If you agree to participate, your panel will, at a time determined by random
assignment, adopt a **provenance scaffolding system**: a software tool that
labels AI-generated summaries with their source, version, and context, and
displays a brief verification prompt for each labelled item.

During the study period (12 weeks), your review activity will be measured by
automated logging of:
- Whether each AI-generated summary was reviewed by a human before a decision
  was made (verification rate τ)
- Whether each summary carried complete provenance attribution (provenance
  fidelity ρ)

A small number of **calibration applications** (5–10% of the review queue)
will be synthetic, created by the research team to measure how reviewers
respond to AI summaries of known quality. These calibration applications
**will not affect real funding decisions** — they are excluded from the
funding pool before the review begins.

---

## Risks

The main risk is minor inconvenience from the verification prompt. There is
no risk of harm to real applicants — calibration applications are identified
and withheld from funding.

---

## Benefits

There are no direct benefits to you. The study may improve understanding of
how to design AI governance systems that keep institutions in the self-healing
coordination regime.

---

## Confidentiality

Your individual review data will be stored on a password-protected server and
accessed only by the research team. Panel-level aggregates (not individual
responses) will be reported in publications.

---

## Voluntary participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time by
notifying your panel chair. Withdrawal will not affect your standing as a
reviewer.
